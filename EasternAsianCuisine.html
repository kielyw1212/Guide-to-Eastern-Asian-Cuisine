Guide to Eastern Asian Food

Introduction

Asia is a big country even when narrowing it down to Eastern Asia. With a big country comes a variety of foods. From Korean bibimbap to to Chinese xiao long bao, Eastern Asia has an abundance of unique foods.

The three most popular cuisines in Eastern Asia are Chinese, Japanese, and Korean food.

China

Because China is so big, there are many different delicious food choices. From authentic Chinese restaurants to even frozen packages, these food items can be found in most Chinese restaurants and supermarkets. Below are some of the most popular food items that are classics to the Chinese cuisine.

1. Dumplings

The first quintessential dish to Chinese culture is dumplings. With its rich history of close to 2,000 years, dumplings have been a favorite throughout China. A meat filling, usually pork, encased in a thin piece of dough makes for a delicious staple to Chinese cuisine. It can be boiled or fried; both ways delicious.

2. Chow Mein

Chow mein is also a very popular dish in China. Chow mein basically means stir-fried noodles. The dish usually is fried with some type of meat, preferablly chicken or pork, onions, and soy sauce. Chow mein is not only popular in China but all over the world. It can be found in many Asian restaurants everywhere.

3. Peking Duck

Peking duck is a traditional meal meant to have with friends in family. It originated from Beijing around 400 years ago. The tasty is made in quite a unique way. Air is pumped into the duck between the skin and meat to ensure the skin is as crispy as possible. The roasted duck is typically served with hoison sauce, sliced cucumbers, scallions, and either a thin pancake-like wrap or steamed buns.

Japan

Japan is another great food destination. Even though Japan is not a large country, the variety in food is still abundant. Many people must have had at least one of these dishes throughout their lifetime because of their popularity throughout the world. Below are some Japanese favorites.

1. Sushi

Sushi is a classic Japanese dish. There are many different kind, ranging from raw to cooked and to rolls or sashimi. However when people say sushi, they typically think of the rolls which are made with rice cooked in vinegar and an assortment of either raw or cooked seafood on top or in the filling. Vegeables are also put into the roll such as cucumber or avocado. It is typically served with pickled ginger, soy sauce, and wasabi.

2. Ramen

Ramen is a dish that is especially delicious in the winter because of the warm soup. This is not to be confused with instant ramen. The noodles are cooked directly in a rich broth and has pork, bean sprouts, green onions, and a soft boiled egg. Their can be many combinations of toppings as well as the soup base flavor.

3. Takoyaki

Takoyaki is a popular Japanese street food. It is a wheat flour ball made in a special mold and usually contains octopus, green onions, and tempura scraps. It is then drizzled with a sauce. Takoyaki has been a popular Japanese dish for many years, starting from Osaka.

Korea

Korean food can be delicious in many different ways from fancy restaurants to simple street food. A lot of people enjoy the following dishes, and they can be found in any classic Korean restaurant. Below are classic Korean dishes that everyone can enjoy.

1. Bibimbap

Bibimbap is probably one of Koreans' favorite comfort food. Bibimbap simply translates to "mixed rice with assorted meats and vegetables". A traditional bibimbap would usually contain raw beef and a raw egg yolk, but it can be made to your dietary restrictions. The dish is then mixed thoroughly before digging in.

2. Korean BBQ

Korean Barbeque is a method in Korean cuisine that basically means grilling the meat yourself. Some popular choices are usually pork, beef, and sometimes chicken like bulgogi (thinly sliced marinated beef), galbi (beef short ribs), and samgyeopsal (pork belly), but there are many other choices too. To accompany the Korean BBQ, there are also many different side dishes such as kimchi and spicy radish.

3. Japchae

Japchae is again a very popular dish throughout Korea and other countries. Japchae is both sweet and savory. The noodles are glass noodles made with sweet potato starch and are then topped with assorted vegetables, meats, and seasoned with sesame oil or soy sauce. This recipe also has a very rich history, dating back to the 17th century.
